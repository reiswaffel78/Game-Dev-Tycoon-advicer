Strategy

Runs fail from misalignment, not one bad choice.

A “good” topic isn’t enough. Genre fit, audience, platform, research order, and slider priorities all need to reinforce each other.

The System

Think in layers, not isolated tips.

Every strong run follows the same structure. Nail each layer and they compound into consistently high review scores.

1
Pick a concept with real synergy.

Topic and genre should amplify each other — not just sound interesting together.

2
Match audience and platform deliberately.

A solid concept still underperforms when the release context doesn’t support it.

3
Set development sliders with intention.

Execution weighting directly impacts review quality. Don’t leave points on the table.

4
Research what helps the next release.

Useful sequencing beats random accumulation of unlocks.

Key Insight

Concept × Execution × Timing

Most guides focus on one piece. But the review score is a product of all three. A weak link anywhere caps your ceiling.

FAQ

Common questions, direct answers.

What is the best way to choose a topic and genre in Game Dev Tycoon?
Evaluate topic and genre as a pair, then check whether your target audience, platform, and development setup support that choice. The Genre Recommender is the fastest way to find strong pairings without trial and error.
Why do my games fail even when the concept sounds strong?
Because concept quality is only one layer. Weak audience fit, poor slider weighting, or badly sequenced research can drag down a good idea. The problem is usually system mismatch — not a single wrong pick.
How do slider settings affect game reviews?
Each genre has optimal design and technology weightings across the three development phases. Misaligned sliders reduce review scores even when the topic–genre combination is strong. Check the Slider Presets for genre-specific setups.
Should beginners use the guide or the tools first?
Skim the guide to understand the logic, then use the tools for actual decisions. More advanced players can jump straight into the Recommender or Planner and come back here when they want the reasoning.
Does research order really matter?
Yes. Research isn’t just about accumulating unlocks — it’s about sequencing them so each one supports your next release. A well-timed unlock gives you points. A poorly timed one wastes them. The Research Guide helps you prioritize.
